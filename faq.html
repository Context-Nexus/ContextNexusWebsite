Frequently Asked Questions

Core Product Usage

Which AI providers are supported?

We officially support OpenAI (ChatGPT / GPT models), Google Gemini, and Anthropic Claude. Additionally, our "Custom Endpoint" option allows you to connect to any OpenAI-compatible API.

ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude are trademarks of their respective owners. Context Nexus is not affiliated with or endorsed by OpenAI, Google, or Anthropic.

Can I use local or self-hosted AI models?

Yes. Our "Custom Endpoint" feature is designed for this purpose. It allows you to connect to any API that is compatible with the OpenAI format, which is ideal for using open-source models running locally (e.g., with Ollama or LM Studio) or connecting to private, self-hosted inference servers.

Which Unreal Engine versions are supported?

Context Nexus is tested and fully supported on Unreal Engine versions 5.1 through 5.4.

Which operating systems are supported?

Currently, the plugin is officially supported on Windows (x64). Support for other operating systems is being evaluated for future releases.

Do I need an internet connection?

Yes. An active internet connection is required to send requests from the editor to your chosen AI provider's API.

Does Context Nexus work with both Blueprints and C++?

Yes, absolutely. This is the core strength of our Dual-Source Analysis Engine, which understands the relationships between your C++ code and Blueprint graphs to provide complete context.

Can I use it in a team project?

Yes. The tool works perfectly in team environments, but each developer using the plugin will need their own license and must connect their own AI provider API key.

Pricing & Licensing

Is there a free trial?

Yes, we offer a 7-day free trial with full access to all features. After the trial period, you will need to upgrade to a paid plan to continue using the tool.

How does licensing work?

Licensing is handled via a key-based activation system on a per-seat basis. One license seat is required for each user.

Do I need a subscription to the AI provider too?

Yes. You are responsible for your own AI provider account (e.g., OpenAI, Google, or Anthropic) and any associated costs based on your usage. We provide the tool; you provide the AI access.

What happens after the trial ends?

Once your 7-day trial is over, the plugin's features will be disabled. You will need to purchase a Pro or Enterprise license to reactivate it.

Privacy & Security

Do you log my code or prompts?

No. We do not see, log, or store your code, prompts, or AI responses. The "Targeted Context" you select is sent directly from your machine to the AI provider's API and is subject to their privacy policy.

Do you store my AI key?

No. Your AI API key is stored securely on your local machine within the editor's settings and is never sent to our servers.

Are you affiliated with OpenAI, Google, or Anthropic?

No. Context Nexus is an independent tool. We are not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by any AI provider. We simply provide a powerful integration that utilizes their public APIs.

Features & Workflow

What’s a "Deep Scan"?

A Deep Scan is the initial analysis step where Context Nexus builds a comprehensive map of your entire project, including all C++ files, Blueprints, and their dependencies. This global understanding is what makes its answers so accurate.

What is "Targeted Context"?

Targeted Context is the specific subset of your project (files, functions, variables) that you select to send to the AI for a particular question. This keeps your requests focused, reduces token costs, and improves the accuracy of the AI's response.

Can I ask about cross-Blueprint and C++ relationships?

Yes. This is a primary feature. You can ask complex questions like, "Which Blueprints will be affected if I change this C++ function?" and get a precise, actionable answer.

Does it warn me about Blueprint performance issues?

Yes, the integrated Static Analysis Linter panel provides early warnings and suggestions for potential performance bottlenecks or common issues found in your Blueprints.

Roadmap

What’s next for Context Nexus?

Our immediate roadmap includes implementing a Clang AST sidecar for compiler-grade C++ parsing, enhancing our dependency analysis engine, and adding support for more AI providers.

Will you support local/offline AI models?

We are actively exploring support for local and offline AI models. While it is not on our immediate roadmap, it is a high-priority feature we are researching for future updates.
